Guest Speakers

AJAY KUMAR SOOD

(Principal Scientific Adviser to the Government of India)

GUEST OF HONOUR & HONORARY DOCTORATE RECIPIENT

Prof. Ajay Kumar Sood is the Principal Scientific Advisor to the Government of India (PSA to GoI) from April 2022.

He is also the Chairperson of the Prime Minister’s Science, Technology & Innovation Advisory Council (PM-STIAC). Prof. Sood is also a National Science Chair at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) Bangalore. He was a member of the Prime Minister’s Science, Technology, and Innovation Advisory Council (PM-STIAC) from 2018 to March 2022 and from 2009 to 2014, he had also served as a Member of the Scientific Advisory Council (SAC) to the Prime Minister of India. Prof. Sood has a Doctorate in Physics from IISc Bangalore. His research interests include the Physics of Quantum materials, soft and active matter. He has published more than 450 papers in peer-reviewed journals and holds several patents. His work has been recognized by many awards, including the Padma Shri, the fourth highest civilian award in India. He was President of the Indian National Science Academy (2017-19), President of the Indian Academy of Sciences (2010-12), and Secretary-General, The World Academy of Sciences (TWAS), Trieste, Italy (2013-2018). He was elected to the Fellowship of the Royal Society, UK (FRS) in 2015.

ROHINI GODBOLE

(Padma Shri Awardee, Indian Physicist,Distinguish visiting Professor of Physics at Shiv Nadar Institution of Eminence)

GUEST OF HONOUR & HONORARY DOCTORATE RECIPIENT

Professor Godbole started her life in physics with a Bachelor’s degree

in Physics, Mathematics and Statistics from Sir Parshurambhau college of Pune with a first rank in the University going to get an M.Sc. in Physics from IIT ( Mumbai) with a silver Medal and then getting a Ph.D. from S.U.N.Y. at Stony Brook in U.S.A in 1979. The latest degree she got was a D.Litt. (Honoris Causa) by the S.N.D.T Women’s University. She really enjoy teaching. Of course she teach at the Indian Institute of Science. The latest set of lectures she gave here were on MSSM phenomenology, a course based on some chapters of her SUSY book. She loves talking about physics and give many talks in the course of an year. Other than doing physics, she loves talking to young students in schools and colleges not just about the world of particle physics but also my other interest viz. increasing women’s participation in Science in India. She gives popular talks about physics and about women in science to adult audiences as well. She has been involved in working for women in science. She loves reading , spending her time watching some of the BBC serialization of John Le Carre books or Father Brown stories or the ageless Columbo!!
